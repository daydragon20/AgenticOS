### 7. AI loops — stop met prompting, schrijf loops (Agent AI, 11 juni)

**Volledig artikel:**

> Boris Cherny, the creator of Claude Code, has given up on prompting AI.
>
> Instead of prompting, they create loops that allow the AI to understand the end goal it's working towards, and when it should stop working.
>
> **"I don't prompt Claude anymore. I write loops — and the loops do the work. My job is to write loops."** — Boris Cherny
>
> **So What Does It Mean to Write a Loop?**
>
> A prompt is a single ask, done by hand. Writing a loop means stepping out of the driver's seat and building the thing that generates those prompts for you. A loop has three parts:
>
> - **Objective** — what you're trying to get the system to accomplish
> - **Metric** — how the system itself can tell whether a given pass came out better or worse
> - **Boundary** — how far the loop can go on its own before it has to come back and check with you
>
> Get those three right, and you stop babysitting every turn: the loop asks, checks its own work against the metric, adjusts, and keeps going without you.
>
> **Example prompt you can steal:**
>
> *Write a LinkedIn post about [your topic]. The objective: teach one useful idea in under 200 words. After each draft, score the result from 1 to 10: does the first line earn the click on "see more", is there exactly one idea, would a smart reader learn something new? For scores below a 9, critique the draft and rewrite it. Take up to 10 passes, then show me only the top 3 winners.*
>
> **A Loop That Runs vs. A Loop That Learns**
>
> A loop that just runs is automation: it does the same thing today that it did yesterday. A loop that learns — the kind that knows whether each pass landed and feeds that signal back in — compounds. It's adaptive.
>
> **Where You Still Belong**
>
> Your job moves out of task execution and into workflow design. You pick the objective, choose the metric, and draw the boundary. The keystroke work goes away (and that's a win), but the judgment work doesn't. The skill that compounds from here is **loop design**.

*Relevant omdat: dit is precies wat jij bouwt met n8n + Claude. Objective → metric → boundary is een direct toepasbaar framework voor jouw agency automations.*
